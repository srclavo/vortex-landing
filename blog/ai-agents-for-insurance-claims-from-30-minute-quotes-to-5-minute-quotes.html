AI Agents for Insurance Claims: From 30-Minute Quotes to 5-Minute Quotes

Insurance claims restoration is one of the most operationally complex businesses for its size. A typical company manages 1,000+ claims per year across multiple carriers (Chubb, GNP, Afirme, Qualitas), each with different requirements, pricing structures, and payment cycles.

The quoting process alone — looking up a catalog of 145+ repair items, calculating quantities based on damage type, referencing historical claims for similar scenarios — takes an experienced estimator 30 minutes per claim.

We embedded AI agents into this workflow. The same estimator now spends 5 minutes.

What Changed (And What Didn't)

The estimator still opens the claim. Still reviews the photos. Still makes the final call on pricing. What changed is everything between opening the file and approving the quote.

The Quoting Agent analyzes the damage photos and the claim description, then cross-references against historical claims with similar characteristics — same carrier, same damage type, same region. It pre-populates the quote with suggested line items, quantities, and unit prices from the catalog. The estimator reviews, adjusts if needed, and approves.

30 minutes of catalog lookup and calculation → 5 minutes of review and approval.

The Photo Classifier automatically categorizes uploaded images: damage assessment, before condition, repair in progress, completed repair. No more manual tagging. The categories feed directly into the claim file and the quoting agent's analysis.

The Collection Agent runs every morning. It scans all open invoices, calculates days outstanding by carrier, and generates a prioritized call list for the AR team. 30-day invoices get a flag. 60-day invoices get an alert. 90+ day invoices get escalated automatically.

Why Embedded Agents Work Here

The key insight: we didn't replace the estimator's system. We didn't ask anyone to learn a new platform. The agents operate inside the existing workflow — the same screens, the same process, the same people.

The estimator doesn't know they're "using AI." They know that when they open a claim, the quote is already 80% done. When they upload photos, they're already categorized. When the AR person arrives in the morning, their call list is already prioritized.

That's what adoption looks like when AI works inside operations instead of on top of them.

The Numbers

  • Quoting time: 30 minutes → 5 minutes per claim
  • Photo categorization: 5 minutes per claim → automatic
  • AR prioritization: 1 hour/day manual review → automated morning briefing
  • Annual claims processed: 1,300+ without adding headcount
  • Collection cycle: 15% improvement in 60+ day recovery

What Makes This Different From "AI Tools"

Generic AI tools ask the claims company to export data, upload it to a new platform, learn a new interface, and maintain two systems. That's why adoption fails in operationally complex businesses — the people doing the work don't have time to learn another tool.

Embedded agents don't require adoption. They require configuration. We study the workflow, identify where intelligence accelerates decisions, and deploy agents that operate within the existing process. The user's experience improves without their workflow changing.
