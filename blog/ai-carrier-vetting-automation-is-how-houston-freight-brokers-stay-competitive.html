The Carrier Vetting Bottleneck Is Costing You Loads

Your team pulls authority status, checks insurance, reviews safety scores, and cross-references past performance. On a good day, that takes 15 minutes per carrier. On a busy day, corners get cut. A bad carrier slips through, a claim follows, and your margin on that load disappears — along with your client's trust.

Mid-size Houston brokerages typically vet 40 to 80 carriers per day. At 15 minutes each, that is 10 to 20 hours of labor spent before a single truck moves. That is not operations — that is overhead.

What AI Carrier Vetting Actually Does

An AI agent pulls authority status, insurance certificates, safety scores, and complaint history in seconds — not minutes. It flags carriers that fail your criteria before a human ever sees them. It cross-references your internal blacklist and preferred carrier database automatically.
