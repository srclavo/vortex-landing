Dental AI Receptionist Comparison 2026: What Actually Matters for Your Practice

Dental AI receptionist platforms hit 15+ options in 2026, and most comparison articles are written by the platforms themselves. Here's what a practice owner actually needs to evaluate before signing anything.

The Market Is Crowded — Most Options Look the Same

DentalBase just published a comparison of 10 platforms. Add the ones they missed and you're looking at 15+ choices ranging from $200 to $600 per month. The names — Arini, Savvy Agents, Annie AI, Dentina, HeyGent, TrueLark (now Weave), Viva AI, Rondah — all promise the same thing: answer calls, book appointments, never miss a patient.

But 73% of dental practices plan to adopt AI tools by 2027. That means the vendors who survive won't be the ones with the best marketing — they'll be the ones whose AI actually works inside your existing systems.

Three Things That Separate Real Solutions from Demos

1. PMS Integration Depth (Read-Only vs. Read-Write)

Most platforms can read your schedule. Fewer can write to it — actually creating appointments in Dentrix, Open Dental, or Eaglesoft without manual re-entry. If your AI receptionist books a patient but someone still has to type it into your PMS, you didn't automate anything.

2. Outbound Capabilities

Answering inbound calls is table stakes. The ROI multiplier is outbound: recall campaigns, reactivation of dormant patients, appointment confirmations, and follow-ups. Practices lose $100K+ annually to no-shows and lapsed patients. An AI that only answers the phone solves half the problem.

3. Compliance and Uptime

Today ChatGPT went down for 90+ minutes globally. If your AI receptionist runs on infrastructure that goes dark during peak hours, you're losing patients. Look for SOC 2, HIPAA BAA, and redundant infrastructure — not just a checkbox on a sales page.
