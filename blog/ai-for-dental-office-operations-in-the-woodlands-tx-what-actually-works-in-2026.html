The Problem: Your Front Desk Can't Scale

A typical dental office in The Woodlands handles 150-200 calls per day. Your front desk team juggles scheduling, insurance verification, patient questions, and walk-ins simultaneously. When call volume spikes — Monday mornings, post-lunch hours — calls go to voicemail. Voicemail means lost appointments. Lost appointments mean lost revenue.

The math is simple. If 15% of your calls go unanswered and each missed appointment is worth $350, you're leaving $15,000-$20,000 on the table every month. That's not a technology problem. It's an operations problem.

Why ChatGPT Alone Won't Fix This

But here's what it doesn't do: answer your phones, schedule appointments across four hygienists, send appointment confirmations, follow up on no-shows, or respond to after-hours messages. Documentation is one piece. Operations is the other 80% of running a practice.
